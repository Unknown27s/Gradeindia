How Target CGPA Works

The Target CGPA calculator uses the reverse CGPA formula to find out what SGPA you need in your next semester:

Required SGPA = (Target CGPA × Total Credits - Current CGPA × Completed Credits) / Next Semester Credits

Example: If your current CGPA is 7.5 with 96 completed credits, and you want a target CGPA of 8.0, with 24 credits next semester: Required SGPA = (8.0 × 120 - 7.5 × 96) / 24 = (960 - 720) / 24 = 10.0

The calculator will tell you if your target is achievable or if the required SGPA exceeds the maximum possible grade point.

Tips to Improve Your CGPA

  • Focus on subjects with higher credit weightage for maximum impact on CGPA
  • Aim for consistency rather than extreme fluctuations between semesters
  • Start early in your degree - it is harder to raise CGPA in later semesters
  • Track your progress regularly using this calculator to stay on target
